# Standard TTS Evaluation Sentences
#
# Collection of phonetically balanced, diverse test sentences for TTS quality validation.
# Includes Harvard sentences, conversational phrases, numbers, and edge cases.
#

# ─── Harvard Sentences (Phonetically Balanced Intelligibility Benchmarks) ───
The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.
Pack my box with five dozen liquor jugs.
The jay, pig, fox, zebra and my wolves quack.
How vexingly quick daft zebras jump.
The five boxing wizards jump quickly.

# ─── Phonetically Diverse Phrases ───
I need to schedule a meeting for tomorrow at two PM.
Can you repeat that again please?
That sounds absolutely wonderful!
We should discuss the quarterly results.
Thank you very much for your help.

# ─── Natural Conversational Patterns ───
How are you doing today?
The weather is beautiful today, isn't it?
Let's meet at the coffee shop around noon.
I'm sorry, I didn't catch that correctly.
What time does the train leave station?

# ─── Numbers & Abbreviations (Edge Cases) ───
One, two, three... ready or not here I come!
Call me at 555-1234 or email test at example.com.
That costs $99.99 for 15% off.
See you at 3:30 PM today.
Dr. Smith, Ph.D., is here.

# ─── Questions & Exclamations ───
Do you think we can finish this project on time?
I can't believe how amazing this sounds!
Would you like to grab coffee next week?
That's incredible! I had no idea.
When are you available for a quick call?

# ─── Technical/Formal Register ───
Please configure the server with the appropriate authentication credentials.
The algorithm demonstrates improved efficiency in distributed computing environments.
According to recent market analysis, quarterly growth exceeded projections.
Our team recommends a phased implementation approach for maximum stability.
The system requires quarterly maintenance to ensure optimal performance.

# ─── Prosody Challenges (Emphasis, Rate Variation) ───
I think you're absolutely right about that decision.
Really? You actually did that yourself?
This is super important, so please pay attention.
Well, it all depends on what you mean by that.
Actually, I've been meaning to tell you something.

# ─── Multi-Clause Sentences ───

Before you make a decision, consider these factors carefully.
If you need help with anything, don't hesitate to reach out to me.
When the project is complete, we should celebrate our achievement together.
Because of these issues, we recommend moving forward with caution.
